Short Answer

Buyers should compare cling film price by total order value and usable product, not by roll price alone. The same roll name can have different width, thickness, length, weight, core, carton quantity, and box structure. A fair comparison aligns all these fields first, then checks landed cost and sample performance before confirming bulk production.

Why Roll Price Alone Can Mislead Buyers

A supplier may quote a low price per roll, but the roll may be shorter, thinner, lighter, or packed in a weaker box. Another supplier may quote a higher roll price but provide longer usable length, stronger cartons, or better cutter box performance. Without aligning specifications, the buyer cannot know which offer is truly cheaper.

This is especially important for importers who resell cling film to restaurants, supermarkets, or local wholesalers. Their customers judge the product during daily use. If the film tears, the carton collapses, or the roll length feels short, the importer may lose repeat business even if the first purchase price looked attractive.

Representative buyer scenario: A wholesaler receives two quotes for 450 mm food wrap. Supplier A gives a lower roll price, but the roll length and carton quantity are different. Supplier B gives full carton dimensions, roll weight, and a stronger cutter box. The buyer cannot compare fairly until both quotes use the same specification format.

Compare by Weight, Length, and Thickness Together

Weight, length, and thickness are connected. A roll with more weight may contain more material, but it may also be thicker or wider. A roll with more length may seem better, but only if thickness and performance still match the buyer's requirement. Buyers should avoid comparing one number without the rest of the specification.

Price per Kilogram vs Price per Roll

Price per kilogram can help buyers compare material cost, especially for jumbo rolls or conversion projects. However, it does not show the full value of finished retail rolls or cutter box products. Finished rolls also include box material, blade, printing, carton packing, labor, and retail presentation.

Price per roll is easier for wholesalers to understand, but it can hide specification differences. A roll price is useful only when width, thickness, length, core size, box type, and carton quantity are already aligned.

Rewinders and converters often compare material cost by weight, then calculate finished roll cost separately.

Use roll price when...

Food-service and retail buyers can compare roll price after all roll and packaging specs are aligned.

You import containers

Importers should include carton volume, freight, duty assumptions, damage risk, and warehouse handling.

Carton Packing Can Change the Real Cost

Carton packing is often underestimated. If a carton is too large, freight efficiency may be poor. If it is too weak, product damage can increase. If carton quantity is different between suppliers, roll price comparisons become confusing. For private-label products, carton marks and retail box protection also matter.

Wholesale buyers should ask for carton dimensions, pieces per carton, gross weight, net weight, pallet plan, and loading estimate when the order is large enough for container shipping. This helps the buyer estimate landed cost and warehouse handling before committing to production.

  • Estimated loading plan for pallet or container orders.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I compare cling film by price per roll or price per kilogram?

Use price per kilogram for material-heavy or jumbo roll comparison. Use price per roll for finished products only after width, thickness, length, core, box, and carton packing are aligned.

Why is one cling film quote much cheaper than another?

The cheaper quote may use different thickness, roll length, roll weight, packaging, carton quantity, or carton strength. Buyers should compare full specifications before judging price.

Does thicker cling film always cost more?

Thicker film usually uses more material, but final cost also depends on width, roll length, packaging, carton packing, order quantity, and freight. Sample performance should also be checked.

How does carton size affect cling film landed cost?

Carton size affects container loading, freight efficiency, warehouse storage, and damage risk. A low unit price can become less attractive if carton volume is inefficient.

What should buyers ask before accepting a low price?

Ask for width, thickness, length, roll weight, core size, packaging details, carton size, carton weight, sample confirmation, and document availability.
